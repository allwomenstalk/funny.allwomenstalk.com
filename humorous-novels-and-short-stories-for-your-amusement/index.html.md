# 7 Humorous Novels and Short Stories That Will Actually Make You Laugh Out Loud

If you don’t believe reading is fun, there are humorous novels and short stories to prove you wrong. In a world that often feels a bit too serious, finding a way to escape into a world of laughter is essential for your mental well-being. The next time that you feel bored or overwhelmed, read through one of the [humorous stories short](https://funny.allwomenstalk.com/humorous-short-stories/) or pick up a copy of a novel at a local bookstore. Either way, you’re sure to be entertained with these humorous novels and short stories that have stood the test of time.

## 1. We Can Get Them for You Wholesale by Neil Gaiman

![We Can Get Them for You Wholesale by Neil Gaiman](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/nx/kg/v34h7s36_578x864.jpg)

You can find the official work at [neilgaiman.com](https://www.neilgaiman.com/). Out of all of the humorous novels and short stories out there, this is one of the greatest, because Neil Gaiman is one of the most amazing writers of our time. This story is about a man who finds out that his partner is cheating on him, and hires someone to kill her. However, the assassin offers a better deal for multiple killings, and the main character doesn’t like to pass up a bargain. It's a perfect example of how [funny novels](https://funny.allwomenstalk.com/funny-novels/) and stories can take a dark premise and turn it into something hilariously absurd.

## 2. I, Robot by Isaac Asimov

![I, Robot by Isaac Asimov](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/nd/q0/man8p3mk_600x994.jpg)

Learn more about the author at [asimovonline.com](https://www.asimovonline.com/). The movie with Will Smith is nothing like the book it’s based on. The book contains multiple short stories, featuring the same set of characters. It shows the progression of robotics, and the paranoia that humans suffer from. On top of that, it is pretty darn humorous. If you appreciate the clever logic found in [The Hitchhiker’s Guide to the Galaxy](#4), you will definitely enjoy the robot logic here.

## 3. Eve's Diary by Mark Twain

![Eve's Diary by Mark Twain](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/vk/wn/jfof6fvu_600x931.jpg)

Read the full text at [gutenberg.org](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/8525). Mark Twain created this short story about Adam and Eve. It’s written in the woman’s point of view, and contains drawings that were a bit controversial at the time. Since the story is formatted like a diary entry, it shows Eve's thought processes during her time in Eden. It's one of those [entertaining short stories](https://funny.allwomenstalk.com/entertaining-short-stories/) that combines wit with a touch of poignancy.

## 4. The Hitchhiker’s Guide to the Galaxy by Douglas Adams

![The Hitchhiker’s Guide to the Galaxy by Douglas Adams](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/of/db/xwwy270x_600x910.jpg)

Visit the official site at [douglasadams.com](https://www.douglasadams.com/). This is a science fiction story that will tell you the answer to life, the universe, and everything. Some crazy events happen, so be ready for the unexpected. If you're too lazy to pick up a book, then you can always watch the movie, but the book is far superior in its wit. It's widely considered one of the best funny novels ever written.

### Why You Should Read More Humorous Stories

- They provide a quick mental break from daily stress and routine.

- Laughter has been scientifically shown to improve mood and reduce anxiety levels.

- Short stories are perfect for busy schedules, offering a complete narrative in just a few minutes.

- They often provide a unique, satirical perspective on human nature and society.

## 5. The Angel of the Odd by Edgar Allen Poe

![The Angel of the Odd by Edgar Allen Poe](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/u1/ld/qsfr1qdj_600x900.jpg)

Explore more at [poemuseum.org](https://www.poemuseum.org/). Everyone knows the name Edgar Allen Poe. What you might not know is that he created more than sad stories. He also created humorous stories, such as this one, although it does contain some of his signature disturbing elements. If you've never read any of his work, then you should start with this gem. It's a great choice if you're looking for something different in the realm of [humorous stories short](https://funny.allwomenstalk.com/humorous-short-stories/).

## 6. The Truth about Pyecraft H. G. Wells

![The Truth about Pyecraft H. G. Wells](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/gn/ix/m1w1vnuj_600x1330.jpg)

If you've ever wanted to lose weight, then you'll find this story comical. It's about a man who uses a weight-loss recipe, only to realize that it literally reduces his weight and not his mass. The rest of the story shows him floating around the room. Even though it was written years ago, it's pretty relatable to how the media acts nowadays, trying to convince customers to buy miracle weight loss products that never work. This story is a classic among [entertaining short stories](https://funny.allwomenstalk.com/entertaining-short-stories/).

## 7. To Serve Man by Damon Knight

![To Serve Man by Damon Knight](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/p6/as/zc90h3zs_600x934.jpg)

This is a science fiction story about aliens coming down to earth. There is an episode of The Twilight Zone that is based on the story, because it's such a great read. Make sure you read it all the way through, because the funniest part doesn't come until the very end. Much like [We Can Get Them for You Wholesale](#1), it uses a twist to deliver its humor.

Stories are meant to take you away from your own life and give you some happiness. These stories are guaranteed to do just that, even if it's only for a few minutes. Whether you are a fan of science fiction or classic satire, there is something on this list for everyone. Do you know of any funny stories that we should read?
